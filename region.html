As can be seen from the chart, there is a large gap between the upper and lower levels of happiness in the Western Europe region, from 4.8 to 7.7 points. As can be seen from the chart, there is a large gap between the upper and lower levels of happiness in the Eastern Asia region, from 4.8 to 6.4 points. Among them, inland areas have relatively low scores, and coastal areas have relatively high scores. And the corresponding GDP per Capita and Health Life Expectancy bar charts also show a positive correlation between these two factors and the happiness index.

As can be seen from the chart, the happiness index in the Middle East and Northern Africa regions ranges from 3 to 7.2 points, with a significant differentiation between the two levels. Some countries in the central region scored higher, while others in the surrounding region scored lower. The corresponding GDP per Capita and Health Life Expectancy bar charts also show a positive correlation between these two factors and the happiness index.

Conclusion of Regions Happiness

As can be seen from the chart of happiness index changes of Western Europe, Eastern Asia, Middle East and Northern Africa from 2015 to 2019, there is a certain gap in the happiness index scores of each region. Most countries in the Western Europe region had higher happiness scores, and a small number had lower happiness scores. In Eastern Asia, happiness scores are concentrated on a scale of five to six. In the Middle East and Northern Africa, happiness scores were almost evenly split between high and low. The corresponding GDP per Capita and Health Life Expectancy bar charts show a positive correlation between happiness and the scores of these two factors. Therefore, it can be learned that the happiness in the hearts of the people and he lives in which region of the earth is not too directly related, happiness feelings more mainly depends on the economic level of the living area and the health level of the individual has a great relationship.
